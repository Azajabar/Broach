BROACH

how to play
1The idea

You're given a handful of numbers and one target. Connect the numbers into a single chain with lines, choosing a + − × or ÷ for each line. Use every number, and make your running total land exactly on the target.

2The one rule that matters

A chain is read strictly left to right, like a pocket calculator — no order of operations, no brackets. So the order you visit the numbers is the puzzle.

tap 3, pick ×, tap 7, pick −, tap 5 — done.
3Playing a board

Tap a number to start your chain (it turns red). Pick an operation from the keys below, then tap the next number to connect it. The little tag on each circle shows your visit order, and the ledger up top keeps a running tally.

↶ undo peels back one step; reset clears the chain. A SOLVED stamp drops when you nail it. Use every number but miss the target, and it tells you what you hit so you can re-route.

4The modes

Daily — one puzzle, the same for everyone, refreshed each day.

Easy — one board, three steps, small numbers (target ≤ 10).

Normal — one board, six steps (target ≤ 50).

Hard — two linked boards. Solve Puzzle A first; its answer drops into the locked 🔗 node of Puzzle B, which you then solve too (target ≤ 100).

Impossible — two linked boards where every number is sealed. Tap a “?” to drop inside it — a whole mini-puzzle hides there. Solve it to reveal the number, then solve the board it sits on.

Pro — you choose the target and how many steps; the game deals a solvable set of numbers.

Party — every number is a random 1-minute mini-game; crack all four and the target appears.

Zen — no timer, no end; solve one and the next quietly appears.

5Tools & tips

Before you pick a difficulty you can switch the stopwatch and a scratch calculator on or off — those choices lock in for the run. The calculator follows normal math order, so it's a true scratchpad, not a cheat for the chain.

Tip: division is the fussy one. Look for it early, while the running total is still small and clean.

6On paper

Write the numbers in a ring, draw a line to each one in the order you visit, label every line with its operation, and keep the running tally in the margin. That's the whole game — no app required.
